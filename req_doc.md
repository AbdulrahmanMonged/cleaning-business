Users:
1. Manager
2. Cleaners
3. Customers
	1. Regular People
	2. Offices

User Stories:
1. as a customer, i want to book an appointment. Pick a date, pick how many hours, tell me the address.  so that i get a cleaner at the date and i don't have to message the manager
2. as a cleaner, i want to know which address and when im heading next. i can press a button says "Start Job". "Leave Job. so that i can go to that job based on my work hours. 
3. as a manager. i want to see everything. Who booked what, which cleaner is going where, if a job got cancelled. And the money — how much came in this week, this month. find a replacement or call the customer if cleaner has a problem. set up their recurring schedule for offices (must be assigned their usually cleaner every week and everyone gets notified). and the one who decides which cleaner goes to which job. so that everything is managed, and less mess happens. less headache

Contradictions Found:
1. Payment, Payment can't be built online, once cleaner presses job done. Cash shall be collected by the cleaner. or send a manual payment link.
2. Slots, customer requests at any time, manager confirms. 
3. Final say on assignments.  

OUT-OF-SCOPE:
1. Availability for cleaners at certain days - the manager knows its staff and assigns manually based on the manager's knowledge
2. Online Payments. not within my stack.
3. Pick preferred cleaner. this will be too much pressure upon a cleaner and won't satisfy both sides (cleaners/customers)

OPEN-QUESTIONS:
1. How client wants to handle exceptions to recurring bookings (public holiday, cleaner sick)


Assumptions:
1. Notifications are being sent via the system
2. Cleaners can send an unavailability request, managers accepts/declines it.
